Zinnia

Zinnias are annuals, so they will grow for one season to produce flowers and seeds, but the original plant will not come back in subsequent years. They have bright, solitary, daisy-like flowerheads on a single, erect stem, which makes them great for use as a cut ting flower or as food for butterflies.

Before you plant zinnias, make sure to understand several zinnia flower facts, such as: Zinnias love the warm temperature Zinnias originated from Central and South America, so they love warmth and light. When planting zinnias, make sure you provide enough natural light (with partial shade in hotter regions).
